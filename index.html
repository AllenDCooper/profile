About Me

Full-stack web development. EdTech. Writing.


Allen Cooper

I am a program manager and full-stack software engineer, with an entrepreneurial spirit and strong leadership skills for bringing teams together to creatively solve customer challenges. I have over eight years of experience in ed tech/educational publishing, and am in the process of completing a 6-month intensive Full Stack Coding Bootcamp at Columbia University’s Fu Foundation School of Engineering (September 2019). In this program, I have built front-end, back-end, and full-stack applications using my technical skills that include HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, JQuery, Node.js, Bootstrap, Express.js, React.js, MongoDB, MySQL, and Database Theory.

I am a graduate of the University of Chicago, where I met my wife Meredith. We now live in West Chelsea in NYC, where I work for Macmillan Learning, leading their College Success and Human Communication programs to develop new products, technologies, and learning materials to be used by hundreds of thousands of college students each year. To me, good engineering is when technology works for us, not the other way around—facilitating our human connections, enhancing our culture, and fueling our self-growth. One of my favorite quotes on this subject is by Alfred North Whitehead:

“Culture is activity of thought, and receptiveness to beauty and humane feeling. Scraps of information have nothing to do with it… We have to remember that the valuable intellectual development is self-development”
– The Aims of Education, 1917

This to me is also the aims of technology.

When I’m not in front of a screen, my hobbies include wine, vegan eats, campfires, and playing guitar—preferably all together!
